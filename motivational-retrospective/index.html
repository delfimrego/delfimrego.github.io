Goal

The goal of this retrospective is to help the team identify what actually motivates them. By getting everyone in the team to understand the intrinsic motivation, they will be able to identify what contributes positively to those factors and what is pushing them down, making Â it easier to decide what needs to be done.

Set the stage

It's important to set an environment where everybody feels safe to speak. Retrospectives are most effective when the team has the right mindset and is focused on self improvement. These Retromat activities are good candidates. Pick the one you feel is most suited for your team.

Gather data

  1. Divide the board into three columns: Autonomy, Mastery and Purpose.
  2. Divide the board into three rows: Glad, Sad and Mad.
  3. Attendees should write one issue per column, placing it on the row representing what they feel about it.

Ask everyone to reflect on the past iteration and think about things related with:

  • Autonomy, the need to be in control. The desire for self direction.
  • Mastery, the need to be good at what we do. The desire to be challenged in a way that we feel itâs achievable even if beyond our current abilities.
  • Purpose, the need to feel weâre an essential part of a larger whole. A sense of purpose gives value and significance to our work.

Then reflect on how it makes them feel:

  • Glad, things appreciated, that motivates positively, that makes you feel glad to be a part of.
  • Sad, things that are not going well and affects being motivated to go on.
  • Mad, things that drives you mad and pushes you down and drain energy from you.

Ask the attendees to share their post-its and explain why they feel this way.

Decide what to do

After some discussion, bring the attendees towards agreement on the major issue(s) that they want to address in their next iteration. Ask them to think about actions that will help them to improve the intrinsic motivators.

Close the retrospective

This stage is important to gather feedback on how the team felt during the activity, so that the format it self can be adapted and improved on future activities.
