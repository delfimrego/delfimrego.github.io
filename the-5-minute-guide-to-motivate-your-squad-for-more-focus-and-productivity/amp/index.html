5 minute guide to motivate your squad for more focus and productivity

Delfim Rego - 13 Dec 2020

Motivation plays an important role on team performance. Given the same technical expertise, a motivated team will achieve better results. Even highly professional capable teams will drop their productivity if the drive is not there.

I've worked in the past with highly capable teams, with amazing technical skills. But they systematically underperformed when did not believe in the product or when they were forced to use outdated tools or processes. Or simply because they did not want to do that job.

How can we motivate teams, and most importantly how to keep a high motivation state?

First we need to understand what is motivation. In the past, motivation was driven mostly by external factors, such as payment, bonuses or promotions. This is extrinsic motivation.
But extrinsic motivators only have positive influence on mechanical or repetitive activities, as research demonstrated since the early 1970s. [1] [2] [3] In fact, extrinsic motivators can even lead to lower performance on tasks requiring creativity, cognitive skills or decision making.

"Workers in a wide variety of jobs are paid based on performance, which is commonly seen as enhancing effort and productivity relative to non-contingent pay schemes. However, psychological research suggests that excessive rewards can, in some cases, result in a decline in performance..." Dan Ariely et al.[4]

However, extrinsic factors can have a negative effect on motivation so make sure your team's salaries are consistent with your industry and geographic area, in order to attract or retain employees.

Science shows that intrinsic factors are the ones to lead to better performance and personal satisfaction. Steven Reiss listed the 16 Basic Desires that motivate our actions and define our personalities. Jurgen Appelo reduced this to 10 intrinsic desires on his CHAMPFROGS model.

But after doing Jurgeb's Moving Motivators game with multiple teams, I believe to be safe to focus on Dan Pink's three most important factors: autonomy, mastery, and purpose.

  • Autonomy, the need to be in control. The desire for self direction. Autonomous teams are more engaged and more proactive.
  • Mastery, the need to be good at what we do. The desire to be challenged in a way that we feel it's achievable even if beyond our current abilities. Maintaining the right level of challenge within the team's tasks is a sure path to creating self learning teams capable of tackling anything.
  • Purpose, the need to feel we're an essential part of a larger whole. A sense of purpose gives value and significance to our work. A team with a clear vision and clear goals will feel inspired and increase productivity.

How these intrinsic factors affect the team's productivity?

Autonomous teams will feel more responsible for their work and will actively react to anything that stands in the way. If a team has the autonomy to decide how to accomplish its tasks, then it will choose the right strategy to accomplish them, thus increasing productivity.

On the other hand, if the team is following blindly a top-down plan, the team will feel "it's not OUR plan, so not our responsibility if does not work". Anything that stands in the way will just be another confirmation bias and yet another complaint about the plan - thus decreasing productivity.

Mastery is directly related with the team's capabilities and challenges presented to them. If the challenge is too easy to accomplish they will become bored, and productivity will decrease - not because they are not able to do it, but because it does not stimulate the will to work. On the other hand, if the challenge is too hard, the team will become anxious and will give up, thus also reducing productivity.

The key is improvement. With effort and persistence, the challenge must be possible in a way that, once completed, the team learned and improved themselves. This will lead the team to a state of flow and engagement that will naturally improve productivity over time.

But mastery and autonomy will not matter if the team lacks a goal, or does not believe in it. Purpose is what drives passion and commitment. A team inspired by a vision, a team that knows what are the benefits of their work will be encouraged to work together towards that goal. Productivity will naturally increase.

What actions can I take to improve motivation?

The actions will depend on the context the team is leaving in. A good approach way to start is to perform the Motivational Retrospective activity. It will identify the major blocking issues your team is facing and provide concrete actions to solve those issues.
